# HR Analytics Dashboard: Attrition & Employee Insights
## Project Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze employee attrition and workforce dynamics to identify the key factors influencing employee turnover. The dashboard aims to help organizations improve employee retention, satisfaction, and workforce planning through data-driven insights.

## Project Description
- This project presents an interactive HR Analytics dashboard that consolidates key workforce metrics such as employee count, attrition rate, salary, and tenure into a single view.
= The dashboard enables analysis of attrition across departments, salary levels, job roles, and work conditions, helping identify patterns and underlying causes of employee turnover. Interactive filters allow users to explore trends and make informed HR decisions.

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Total Employees
- Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate (%)
- Average Salary
- Average Years at Company

## Additional metrics include:
- Attrition by department
- Attrition by salary slab
- Job satisfaction levels
- Work-life balance impact
- Overtime analysis

## Project Process
## 1️.Data Collection
The dataset includes employee-level information such as:
- Department and job role
- Salary and income groups
- Years at company
- Job satisfaction and work-life balance
- Overtime and commute distance
- Attrition status
  
## 2.Data Cleaning & Preparation
To ensure data accuracy, the dataset was preprocessed:
- Removed null and missing values
- Checked for duplicate records
- Standardized categorical fields (department, job role, etc.)
- Verified and corrected data types and inconsistencies
- The dataset required basic cleaning and validation before analysis.

## 3.Data Analysis
- Analyzed attrition trends across departments and job roles
- Evaluated the impact of salary, overtime, and work-life balance
- Studied employee behavior based on experience and commute distance
- Calculated key HR metrics such as attrition rate and average tenure
  
## 4️.Dashboard Development
The dashboard was built using interactive visualizations:
- KPI Cards for key HR metrics
- Bar & Column Charts for department and salary analysis
- Pie/Donut Charts for attrition distribution
- Slicers for dynamic filtering
- Visuals highlighting employee satisfaction and workload patterns

## Project Insights
- Certain departments show higher attrition rates, indicating workload or role-related challenges
-  Employees in lower salary ranges are more likely to leave, highlighting compensation concerns
- Overtime and poor work-life balance strongly correlate with higher attrition
- Early-career employees and those with long commute distances tend to have higher turnover
